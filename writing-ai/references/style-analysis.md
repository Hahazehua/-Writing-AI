# Style Analysis Rubric

Create a short task-local profile from evidence repeated across multiple documents.

Analyze:

- Perspective: first person, observer, argumentative, autobiographical, or mixed
- Opening: event, question, personal feeling, quotation, or direct thesis
- Structure: concrete experience to abstraction, opposing views, causal analysis, and conclusion
- Reasoning: how the writing handles uncertainty, tradeoffs, responsibility, systems, and human motives
- Rhythm: sentence length, paragraph length, repetition, pauses, and escalation
- Transitions: recurring connective phrases and question patterns
- Vocabulary: abstract concepts, emotional terms, English insertions, and level of formality
- Tone: reflective, idealistic, critical, restrained, compassionate, or confrontational
- Ending: action, belief, unresolved question, aphorism, or return to the opening image

Use only patterns supported by several passages. Avoid copying memorable sentences, private names, personal details, or topic-specific claims into unrelated articles.

Improve readability while retaining voice. Correct obvious typos, duplicated wording, broken grammar, and unsupported factual claims.
